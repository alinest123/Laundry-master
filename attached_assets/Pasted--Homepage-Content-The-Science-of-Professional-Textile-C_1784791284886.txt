## On Google search visibility (your technical SEO note)

Agreed on the framing — no developer or writer can guarantee first-page rankings, and any claim otherwise is a red flag. What actually moves the needle, in priority order for a site at your current stage:

1. **Fix the content-depth gap first.** Eight articles can't support 20 topics or real topical authority. This matters more than any technical fix right now.
2. **Structured data** — Article schema on every post, Organization schema sitewide, FAQ schema where genuinely relevant (not stuffed in for the sake of it).
3. **Internal linking** — every article should link to its pillar page and 2-3 related articles; right now with 8 articles this is thin by necessity, but the pattern should be set from article #1.
4. **Core Web Vitals / mobile performance** — verify with PageSpeed Insights once the site is live; the placeholder image blocks in the hero suggest some pages may not be final.
5. **Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools** — submit sitemap as soon as the brand/URL structure is finalized, not before, since changing slugs after indexing costs you.
6. **Backlinks** — will come naturally from genuinely original research (e.g., real fabric testing data, real case studies) faster than any outreach campaign will get you there artificially.

No individual technical fix will outrun the content-depth problem. That's the real bottleneck right now.
